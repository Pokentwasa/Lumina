COMMERCIAL — FRANSCHHOEK, WESTERN CAPE — 2023

The GlassPavilion

ClientPrivate Wine Estate
LocationFranschhoek, Western Cape
Year2023
ScopeArchitecture, Interior, Landscape
Area340 m² internal + 600 m² terraced gardens

Architecture asframe, not object.

The Glass Pavilion is a tasting room and event space for a boutique wine estate in Franschhoek. The brief called for something that could host intimate tastings of 12 people and formal dinners for 80 — a structure flexible enough to serve both without compromising either. The valley views were the most important feature on the site. Everything else came second.

The result is a structure that barely exists. A single-pitch glass roof spanning 22 metres, supported by slender steel columns at 6-metre intervals, with three elevations fully glazed. In summer the glass walls fold open completely; in winter they close to create a heated greenhouse effect that makes it the warmest room on the estate.

THE CELLAR INTEGRATION

Below the surface.

Beneath the pavilion, a new 120m² cellar was carved directly into the shale hillside. Temperature-stable at 14°C year-round without mechanical intervention. The cellar is accessible from the pavilion floor via a frameless glass hatch — visible during tastings as a theatrical reveal, a literal window into the wine below the guests' feet. A stone staircase descends into the cool dark, lined with the estate's reserve collection.

PROJECT SPECIFICATIONS

Structure

Steel portal frame, 22m single span, painted obsidian

Glazing

Fully automated folding glass walls, triple-glazed roof panels

Cellar

120m² rock-cut cellar, passive temperature control, 14°C constant

Floors

Reclaimed French limestone, sealed for outdoor use

Terracing

600m² stone-walled terraced gardens, indigenous Cape planting

Capacity

12 (seated tasting) to 80 (formal dinner), 120 (cocktail standing)
