RESIDENTIAL — CLIFTON, CAPE TOWN — 2024

The ObsidianHouse

ClientPrivate Residential
LocationClifton, Cape Town
Year2024
ScopeArchitecture, Interior Design, Landscape
Area620 m²

A residence definedby what it removes.

The Obsidian House sits on a dramatic Clifton clifftop, cantilevered over the Atlantic with views that demanded restraint rather than spectacle. The brief was simple: disappear into the landscape. The solution was black — raw basalt cladding, charred timber screens, and floor-to-ceiling glass that makes the structure read as shadow rather than mass.

Every room is oriented toward the ocean. Natural ventilation eliminates the need for mechanical cooling. The thermal mass of the stone walls absorbs heat through the day and releases it at night, keeping interior temperatures stable year-round.

DESIGN INTENT

Material truth.

The material palette was drawn from a single rule: nothing that couldn't be found within 200km of the site. Black slate quarried from the Cederberg. Reclaimed yellowwood from a demolished Woodstock warehouse. Bronze hardware oxidised by hand. The result is a house that feels ancient and inevitable — as if it were always there, waiting to be uncovered.

PROJECT SPECIFICATIONS

Structure

Reinforced concrete cantilever, 14m span over cliff face

Cladding

Raw basalt panels, hand-laid. Charred timber screens (Shou Sugi Ban)

Glazing

Triple-glazed structural glass, floor to ceiling, 3.4m high

Interior

Honed black slate floors, reclaimed yellowwood joinery, bronze fixtures

Landscape

Fynbos endemic planting, no irrigation required post-establishment

Energy

12kW solar array, rainwater harvesting, passive cooling throughout
