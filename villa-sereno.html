VILLA — LAKE COMO, ITALY — 2024

VillaSereno

ClientPrivate Family Residence
LocationLake Como, Lombardy, Italy
Year2024
ScopeArchitecture, Interior, Garden
Area880 m² + 2,400 m² garden

Italian restraint.African soul.

Villa Sereno began as a 19th-century stone farmhouse on the eastern shore of Lake Como — structurally sound but architecturally frozen. The South African family who commissioned us wanted a family compound that honoured the original fabric while making it fully liveable for a contemporary family of eight, with spaces for extended stays and occasional rental use.

Our approach was archaeological rather than renovative. We mapped every original stone course, every timber joint, every hand-made brick before designing a series of interventions that would be legible as new without being at war with what was already there. New volumes in raw concrete and bronze-framed glass contrast deliberately with the original ochre stone.

THE GARDEN

Designed to agebeautifully.

The 2,400m² garden was designed in collaboration with Milan-based landscape architect Elena Ferri. The formal parterre closest to the villa references the original Italian garden layout discovered in 19th-century survey drawings. Beyond it, the planting becomes progressively wilder — transitioning from clipped hedgerow to naturalistic lakeside meadow over 80 metres of terracing.

PROJECT SPECIFICATIONS

Original Structure

19th century, Lecco stone masonry, entirely retained and restored

New Additions

Board-formed concrete, bronze-framed glazing, standing seam copper roof

Interiors

Venetian plaster, Pietra Serena stone, hand-knotted textiles by Cape Town artisans

Pool

Infinity edge overlooking lake, natural stone finish, 18m × 4m

Garden

2,400m² formal and naturalistic, lake-fed irrigation, endemic planting

Sustainability

Geothermal heating, solar array concealed in new copper roof, greywater recycling
